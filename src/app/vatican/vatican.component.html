The Vatican

Pope Clement XV has had much to contend with since his recent elevation to Bishop of Avignon and leadership of the Holy Roman Catholic Church, but foremost in his plans has been re-establishing the influence of the Papacy in Venice. There are a number of reasons for this, some mundane but most due to the city’s unique position to exploit the Rent in the Sky and the magic it brings.

Cardinal Gerdil’s Rise

Clement was formerly Cardinal Gerdil, Inquisitor General of the Vatican, and instrumental in recent events that have beset the world. He was one of the few Cardinals to have access to the Herald during his lessons in magic, and it was on his orders that copies of the book taken from this dictation were made. These grimoires are at the centre of a small but powerful sect at the heart of the Vatican, dedicated to unlocking the secrets of magic and retaining them purely for the benefit of mother church. It was doubtless Gerdil’s order that sealed the fate of the Herald by an assassin’s blade and set in motion the terrible events that saw the Vatican destroyed, Italy torn asunder, and the world forever changed. Gerdil left the Vatican days before the horrific events of the Rent, along with a select few members of his secretive group. Fearing retribution from the Church for his actions, the Cardinal had no idea of the divine reckoning that the murder of the Herald would bring. With Italy opened up to the heavens and earth itself, Cardinal Gerdil and his party were lucky to survive the cataclysm, living for weeks from village to village, barely escaping the rioting and madness that followed. Northern Italy had become a wasteland, beset by water and lava, leaving very few places for Gerdil to seek sanctuary. Luckily he heard tell of a papal conclave, the remaining power of the Church having moved to Switzerland. The Cardinal and his retinue fled to Geneva, protected as it was from the flooding by the Alps, and arrived in time for the meeting. Cardinal Gerdil and his retinue were among only a tiny number of the Vatican to survive the opening of the Rent in the Sky. Cardinal Antonelli was known to have survived, and others still had escaped the disaster, retreating with knowledge of the Herald’s teachings, and even a few transcribed scriptures. Gerdil had a short list of those he believed to have survived, but was keen to hear and meet any others that escaped, lest the knowledge of the Harbginer’s existence spread. Equally the papal conclave was eager to hear of his escape, and saw the magical gifts he showed as proof of God’s existence in the world. Gerdil told of how he had emerged from the flaming ruins of Vatican City, chosen by God as his messenger on Earth. Gerdil took his opportunity to explain of the corruption present in Rome, detailing the sins of the Cardinals and Bishops that lead the Holy Lord to smite the whole place from existence. His retinue did not correct the untruths, convinced as they were of Gerdil’s piety. They knew that only the Cardinal could lead the Church in this new world, and as he spoke, many started to believe the lies he was telling, the words overwriting their own memory of the horrors they’d witnessed in the past weeks. On November 22nd, Cardinal Gerdil was elected as Pope Clement XV.

Play Style

The Vatican are very tough to beat, with a very defensive style with great defensive buffs they can hold their own against any assualt from the other factions. They have strong magical abilities and a few aces up their sleeves when they absolutely need to kill someone. A Prelate of the Flaming Sword gives a fantastic bonus to allies to get through tough armour, and the Exorcist can make sure opposing mages stay hidden. lest they be purified by his holy righteousness.
