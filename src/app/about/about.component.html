Playing Carnevale

Carnevale is a narrative miniatures game set in Venice after a catastrohic event. A world of magic, monsters and daring heroism is bought to life on the tabletop by you and your opponent. Bloody battle, spectacular stunts through the rooftops and water, maddening monsters and non stop action is all represented on your tabletop. The rules allow a great deal of freedom in movement and in combat allowing you to truly build your own narrative. There are currently seven factions with Venice, all fighting for superiority, or to further their own, nefarious agendas. Each faction has it's very onw style, both in looks and how they they play. To be succesful you need to master the playstyle of your chosen faction, making the most from every character in your gang.

Further information on each faction is avaialble on this website, including history, key characters and a quick look at their playstyle. For each faction there is a faction starter gang box and it is also possible to buy the miniatures seperately, or via other gang boxes. A gang is made up of characters from one faction only, unless they have hired the help of a Gifted character. Although there are rules for building a gang within the Carnevale rulebook, it is perfectly acceptable for you and your opponent to modify or change these rules for yourselves to create your own narrative.

One of the most unique and exciting features of Carnevale is how the dice determine your fate. When rolling dice you always roll a destiny dice. This dice is like any other, unless it rolls a 1 or a 10, in which case it could determine a critical or a fumble, a fantastic success, or a terrible mistake. This way there is a feeling of destiny in your actions, and that helps bring the story to life.

Every Carnevale character has a set of stats and abilities which determine their skills, strengths and weaknesses. It's these stats that determine how good your character is at what you want them to do. Let's have a look at them:

  • Name: The characters name.
  • Base Size: What size base the character uses, this determines the characters size.
  • Cost: Price in Ducats of this character.
  • Keywords: What keywords the character has, these are important for certain special rules, identifying the type of character and so on.

    • The next stats are used to determine the characters strength on the tabletop.

  • MOV: Movement, how far the character can move when making a move action.
  • DEX: Dexterity, the characters ability to cimb, dodge and so on. Higher the number, the more mobile the character is.
  • ATT: Attack, how many dice the character rolls when attacking.
  • PRO: Protection, how many dice the character can roll to protect themselves from damage.
  • MIND: Mind points, how strong the characters mental strength is for casting spells and other abilities.

    • These next stats are finite for eaach character, once used, they are used.

  • Action Points: Is refreshed every activation, how many actions a character can make each turn.
  • Life Points: How much health a character has, once these are taken down to 0 the character is dead.
  • Will Points: How much will the character has, Will Points can be used to add extra dice to certain rolls.
  • Command Points: Used when the character uses a command ability.

    • The next stats make the character unique in various ways:

  • Special Rules: Many characters have a list of Special Rules that give them certain skills in the game. A number denotes how strong this rule is for the character.
  • Unique Rules: The characters unique rules, many characters have unique abilties as described here.

    • Next is the weapon Stats and rules:

  • Weapon: Name of the weapon.
  • RNG: Range of this weapon.
  • EVD: Evade score, hiw easy or difficult this weapon is to evade.
  • DMG: How much extra damage this weapon can cause.
  • PEN: Penetration, how well this weapon goes through armur and other means of protection.
  • Abilities: Special rules and abilities for this weapon.
