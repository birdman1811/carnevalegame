The Rashaar

The Rashaar are a race of creatures from beyond the pale. They are an ancient race, older than the realms of mankind by millennia. The creatures are undoubtedly linked to the emergence of the Rent in the Sky, and their occupation of Venice is no mere coincidence. However, their emergence pre-dates the great catastrophe both in the City of Canals by dozens of years, and on the Earth by many thousands.

Ancient Civilisations

There are tales that stretch back hundreds if not thousands of years, back to the dawn of man. Since man first laid eyes on the vast oceans of the world, tales have been told of the creatures that live under the waves. Myths span cultures of ships swallowed by the oceans and the spirits of drowned sailors being reborn as serpentine aquatic beasts. Throughout history rumours have spread of vast creatures that have grown gargantuan over aeons of living in the deepest depths of the ocean, coming to the surface rarely but leaving none but the bravest or luckiest sailors alive to tell the tale. Older still though are stories of vast cities, those that are both ancient and somehow far advanced from humankindâ€™s own technology. Cities that lie in the centre of oceans, standing tall as beacons of a race that came before. Stories abound of cities covered in marble, towering heights that glitter in the sun, or cities paved with gold, covered in elaborate carvings and statues. The tales all end the same way, with the cities falling beneath the water, broken and sunken beneath the waves, destined to lay at the bottom of the oceans forever. There are many variations on the stories, some claiming that God himself is responsible for punishing the inhabitants, others saying that the cities collapsed under the weight of their own splendour, consumed by excess and extravagance. These stories, as with most, have truths in the heart of them. The monsters living beneath the seas are from times before humans, but are not of this world. And the cities may once have been glittering, but in truth were never on top of the water, existing always at the very bottom of the oceans. These places were the first bastions of the Rashaar, and the sea monsters are the only remnants of a race of creatures that long ago dwindled to but a handful across the globe.

Play Style

The Rashaar have a good selection of magic users, magical monsters and a variety of small monsters and humans that assist them. An extremely balanced faction that can be used in a variety of ways. You may not have many ranged attacks without magic, but that is well made up for by their viciousness in close combat and their magical ability. The Rashaar have access to the biggest, scariest monster in the game with the Morgraur, a monster cpable of devouring even powerful creatures whole.
